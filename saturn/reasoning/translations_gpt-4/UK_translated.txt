The Falcon 9 is an American launch vehicle developed by SpaceX. The
rocket is designed for humans, has two stages and runs on liquid fuel.
Since 2010, it has been used for a variety of missions, including
commercial satellite launches, cargo deliveries to the International
Space Station and manned flights.  As of 2023, the Falcon 9 is one of
the main rockets launching cargo into low Earth orbit (LEO). Falcon 9
is one of the leaders in the number of successful LEO launches.  The
Falcon 9 has a maximum payload capacity of 22,800 kg to LEO, or 8,300
kg to Geostationary Transfer Orbit (GTO).  The Falcon 9 was developed
under the leadership of Elon Musk at SpaceX's headquarters in
Hawthorne, California. In total, more than 100 Falcon 9 rockets have
been built and launched. They have been used for a variety of
missions, including the launch of NASA's first commercial manned
mission, Demo-2.
