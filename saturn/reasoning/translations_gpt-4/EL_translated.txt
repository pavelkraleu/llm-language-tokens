Falcon 9 is an American heavy vehicle launcher developed by SpaceX.
The rocket is partially reusable, has two stages and is powered by
liquid fuel. It has been in service since 2010 and has been used for
many manned and unmanned missions, including the ISS space station.
Since 2023, Falcon 9 has carried humans to the International Space
Station (ISS) and has performed many successful missions. Falcon 9 has
the capability to launch cargo into low Earth orbit, with a maximum
payload of 22,800 kg for low Earth orbit missions. Falcon 9 was
designed and built by California-based SpaceX. It was the company's
first rocket capable of carrying humans into space. It was launched
many times, with considerable success, including several manned
missions to the International Space Station (ISS).
