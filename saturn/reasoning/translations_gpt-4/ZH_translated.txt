The Falcon 9 is a two-stage orbital launch vehicle developed by the
American space company SpaceX for commercial and government launch
missions and is still in service. The rocket is manned and powered by
liquid fuel. From 2010 to the present, it has carried out several
manned missions and launched a number of United States commercial and
military satellites.  As of 2023, Falcon 9 has been sending payloads
to low Earth orbit (LEO) and beyond. Falcon 9 has a payload capacity
of about 22,800kg (to LEO), including the manned Dragon spacecraft and
a variety of commercial satellites.  The Falcon 9 is SpaceX's
workhorse rocket, designed under the direction of Elon Musk at
SpaceX's headquarters in California, with SpaceX's own prime
contractors. To date, multiple launch vehicles have been built and
launched without loss of personnel or payload. From its first launch
in 2010 to the present, Falcon 9 has provided launch services to
multiple international and commercial customers.
