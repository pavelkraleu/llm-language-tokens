Falcon 9 is a currently active American super heavy-lift launch
vehicle developed by SpaceX under various programs for human and cargo
exploration. The rocket is human-rated, has three stages, and is
powered with liquid fuel. Flown from 2010 to present, it has been used
for numerous crewed and uncrewed missions to various destinations,
including the International Space Station (ISS) and satellite
launches. As of 2023, Falcon 9 has successfully carried humans and
cargo beyond low Earth orbit (LEO) multiple times. Falcon 9 holds
records for the heaviest payload launched and largest payload capacity
to LEO: 50,300 lb (22,800 kg), which included the Dragon spacecraft
and unburned propellant needed to send payloads to their intended
destinations. The largest production model of the Falcon family of
rockets, Falcon 9 was designed under the direction of Elon Musk at
SpaceX's facilities in Hawthorne, California; the lead contractors
were SpaceX. A total of numerous flight-capable vehicles have been
built, with ongoing production for future missions. Most launches
occur from Cape Canaveral Space Force Station and Kennedy Space Center
with a high success rate. Numerous astronauts and cosmonauts have been
launched to the ISS using Falcon 9 since its first crewed mission in
2020.
