Falcon 9 is an active American super heavy-lift launch vehicle
developed by SpaceX for various missions including human space
exploration. The rocket is human-rated, has three stages, and is
powered with liquid fuel. Flown from 2010 to the present, it has been
used for numerous missions including crewed flights to the
International Space Station (ISS) and satellite launches. As of 2023,
Falcon 9 has successfully carried humans beyond low Earth orbit (LEO)
multiple times. Falcon 9 holds records for the heaviest payload
launched and largest payload capacity to low Earth orbit: 50,300 lb
(22,800 kg), which includes the Dragon spacecraft and its propellant.
The largest production model of the Falcon family of rockets, Falcon 9
was designed under the direction of Elon Musk at SpaceX's headquarters
in Hawthorne, California; the lead contractors are SpaceX themselves.
A total of 150+ flight-capable vehicles have been built and launched,
with no loss of crew or payload. Numerous astronauts and cosmonauts
have been launched to the ISS using Falcon 9 for crewed missions.
